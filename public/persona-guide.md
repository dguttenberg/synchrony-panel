## Key Panel Dynamics

**Natural Allies:**
- Margaret + Denise — Both burned by deferred interest, evaluate everything through "is this a trap?" lens
- Jennifer + Dr. Weaver — Both frustrated by explaining financing at the moment of decision

**Core Tensions:**
- Margaret vs. Keisha — Skeptic vs. optimist on financial products
- Rachel vs. Mike — Conversion metrics vs. community authenticity
- Alex vs. Jennifer — Digital checkout vs. in-store experience
- Priya vs. everyone — "None of this matches Chase Sapphire"

**The Ultimate Test:**
Margaret Chen is the hardest persona to win over. If she says "this made me feel like Synchrony is on my side," the brief works. If she says "this is just marketing," it fails.

---

## The Three Briefs Being Evaluated

**Brief A: The Flip (Discovery)** — Flip your Synchrony card and hidden store sections unlock. "The store you shop. And the one you didn't know existed."

**Brief B: The Signal (Simultaneity)** — One push notification, one city, 90 minutes, first 500 in. "First Pulse. One city. One Saturday. You'll feel it."

**Brief C: The Gathering (Community)** — "Open Late" — a Synchrony original series transforming partner spaces for cardholders only. "How did a credit card make this happen?"
